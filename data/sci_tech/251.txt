obit: baruch blumberg, nobel winner and extraterrestrial researcher
baruch blumberg, who won the 1976 nobel prize in medicine for his work identifying the hepatitis b virus and in the 1970s helped create a vaccine against the disease that is credited with saving millions of lives, died tuesday during a workshop at a nasa workshop in california. he shared the prize with d. carleton gajdusek for the duo's work on the origins and spread of infectious viral diseases.
